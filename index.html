What makes grim different?

We have a 1:1 replication of the player's possible movements that cover everything from walking, swimming, knockback, cobwebs, bubblecolumns, pigs, boats, striders, horses... and much more

We account for minor version differences such as ignoring waterlogged blocks when a 1.12 player is on a 1.13 server

All movement checks are run off of the main thread and the packet thread, on all versions. Note that some competitors claim asynchronous but aren't really on 1.13+, such as Vulcan and AAC. Grim is truly asynchronous due to the world replication

We are also multithreaded, as well as async - meaning we can theoretically scale to many hundreds of players.

The world is replication with packets as to not slow down the main thread. Chunks are compressed to 24 kb per chunk. All blocks are lag compensated on an individual player basis, including block placing, breaking, door opening, pistons, flowing water... and so on
